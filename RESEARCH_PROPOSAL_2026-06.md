## Rationale

Analysis of 28 AI Infrastructure Engineer postings from 2026-03 to 2026-05 confirms the existing 10 modules already cover the universal table-stakes (Python, Linux, Docker, Kubernetes, cloud, Terraform, CI/CD, observability, GPU, MLOps, data pipelines, LLM serving with vLLM/Triton, RAG, vector DBs, fine-tuning, cost optimization). The single material shift in the last 90 days that the mid-level (L20) AI Infrastructure Engineer role genuinely owns is the rise of *AI agent infrastructure* — production platforms that host, orchestrate, observe, and govern multi-agent and MCP-based systems. Capstone explicitly lists Model Context Protocol implementations; Yuno's posting is literally titled 'AI Agent Infrastructure'; Autohive's product is multi-agent; Scale AI's enterprise platform calls out 'secure, scalable Agentic workflows.' This is L20 work because it sits on top of the K8s/cloud/observability stack the engineer already owns — not deep performance tuning (L35), not multi-cloud architecture (L30+), not leadership (L40+). Other emerging trends — LLM-D / disaggregated serving (L35 Performance), Slurm+K8s hybrid orchestration (L30 ML Platform), InfiniBand/RDMA tuning (L35 Performance) — are linked from JOB_REQUIREMENTS.md to their natural higher-level owners rather than duplicated here. No additions are proposed for those.
